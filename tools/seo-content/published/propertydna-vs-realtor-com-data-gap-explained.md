## The Five Data Categories Listing Platforms Consistently Skip

- Permit history and unpermitted work: Realtor.com shows square footage. It doesn't tell you whether that finished basement or extra bedroom was ever permitted — a gap that can mean $15,000–$40,000 in remediation costs or forced deconstruction after purchase.
- Flood zone designation and claim history: A property can sit in FEMA Special Flood Hazard Area Zone AE and carry mandatory flood insurance requirements without a single mention on its listing page. PropertyDNA surfaces FEMA flood zone data at the parcel level.
- Code violations and open liens: Active municipal code violations or outstanding tax liens don't show up in listing data. They attach to the deed and become your problem the moment you close.
