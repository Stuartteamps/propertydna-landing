## What the Redfin Estimate Actually Measures (And What It Misses)

The Redfin Estimate is an automated valuation model, or AVM. It ingests MLS sales data, tax records, and listing details to produce a single number. For a home currently listed on the MLS with a clean history and lots of nearby comps, it can be reasonably useful as a sanity check. The problem is everything it cannot see. Unpermitted additions — a finished basement, a converted garage, an accessory dwelling unit added without permits — inflate a home's apparent square footage without adding legitimate appraised value. The Redfin Estimate will price that square footage as if it were legal. You will pay for it at closing or lose it entirely when your lender's appraiser flags it.

- Redfin Estimate does not surface open or expired building permits
- It does not flag FEMA flood zone classifications like AE, VE, or X-500
- It cannot detect title clouds, liens, or code enforcement actions
- It relies on MLS data, which sellers and agents control
- Its off-market accuracy degrades significantly — error rates above 6% are common
- It is optimized to keep you engaged on Redfin's platform, not to protect your purchase

## How PropertyDNA Approaches Valuation Differently

PropertyDNA does not just ask what similar homes sold for. It asks why this specific property is or is not worth what the seller is claiming. That context does not appear anywhere in the Redfin Estimate.

## The Accuracy Problem: Why AVMs Fail Buyers in 2026

The 2025-2026 market has created a particularly dangerous environment for AVM-dependent buyers. In markets where inventory has tightened sharply — parts of Florida, the Carolinas, and inland California — there are fewer recent comparable sales for any AVM to draw on. When an AVM has fewer than five solid comps within half a mile in the last 90 days, its confidence interval explodes. Redfin's own published methodology notes that its accuracy is highest in dense urban markets with high sales volume. If you are buying in a suburb, an exurban community, a waterfront market, or anywhere with irregular lot sizes or mixed property types, you are operating well outside the conditions where these estimates hold up. PropertyDNA's approach uses hyper-local price-per-square-foot trend lines and permit-adjusted square footage — so the baseline for comparison is not just what sold nearby, but what was legally permitted to sell nearby.
