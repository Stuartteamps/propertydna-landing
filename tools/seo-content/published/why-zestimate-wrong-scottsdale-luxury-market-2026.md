# Why Zestimate Is So Wrong in Scottsdale's Luxury Market in 2026

Zestimate is wrong in the Scottsdale luxury market because Zillow's algorithm is built for volume — it needs dense, frequent, comparable sales data to produce an accurate number. Scottsdale's luxury tier, roughly defined as homes priced above $2 million, gives it almost none of that. ## How Zillow's Algorithm Actually Works — and Where It Breaks Down

Zillow's Zestimate uses a neural-network model trained on publicly recorded sales data, tax assessments, listing histories, and user-submitted details like square footage and bed-and-bath counts. It calculates value by comparing a subject property to recent nearby sales and weighting those comparables by similarity and recency. In a suburb where 200 three-bedroom ranches sold in the past six months, this is a reasonable proxy for value. In Scottsdale's luxury corridors — Silverleaf, Estancia, Whisper Rock, McDowell Mountain Ranch's upper tier — it collapses. The model may draw on comparables from neighborhoods a mile away that share zip codes but nothing else in terms of lot size, architectural quality, or amenity access. It cannot read a view, score a sub-zero appliance package, or account for the fact that a home backs to the 14th fairway at TPC Scottsdale.

## The Off-Market Problem: Scottsdale's Luxury Sales That Zillow Never Sees

A significant slice of Scottsdale's luxury transactions — industry estimates suggest anywhere from 20 to 35 percent of closings above $3 million in North Scottsdale — happen off-market, sometimes called 'pocket listings' or agent-to-agent deals. These sales are eventually recorded in public deed records, but they often never appear in the MLS with full detail. Zillow ingests MLS data as its primary source. When a $5.2 million sale in Estancia closes as a private transaction and surfaces in county records with minimal detail, Zillow either misses it entirely or captures it without the condition, renovation, and feature data needed to use it as a reliable comparable. The result: the algorithm's mental picture of what luxury Scottsdale homes are worth is based on an incomplete dataset by definition.
