## AVM Accuracy: What Both Platforms Get Right and Wrong

Automated valuation models have real limitations regardless of who builds them. HouseCanary publishes a median absolute percentage error (MdAPE) that has historically been competitive — often cited in the 3-5% range for well-traded markets with sufficient comps. PropertyDNA's valuation engine is calibrated to flag confidence levels explicitly, telling you when an AVM estimate is derived from thin comparable data versus a dense comps environment. Why does that matter? Because a 5% error on a $600,000 home is $30,000 — more than most buyers have in closing cost reserves. Knowing whether to trust the number is as important as seeing the number. PropertyDNA surfaces that uncertainty instead of hiding it behind a polished interface.
