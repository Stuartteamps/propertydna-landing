## Historic Preservation Rules: What You Can and Cannot Do to a Vista Las Palmas Home

This is where buyers get blindsided. The City of Palm Springs has one of the most active historic preservation programs in California, and Vista Las Palmas sits in the middle of it. Approximately 180 individual properties within the neighborhood have some form of Class 1 Historic Site designation or are contributors to the Palm Springs Historic Resources Survey. If you buy a designated home, you are required to obtain a Certificate of Appropriateness from the Historic Site Preservation Board before making exterior alterations — and that includes paint colors, windows, doors, roofing materials, and any addition visible from the street. Interior work is generally unrestricted, but the moment your renovation touches the exterior envelope, you are in a review process that can add four to twelve weeks and real cost to a project. Buyers who plan a significant remodel need to budget for preservation-compliant materials, which can run 20 to 35 percent more than standard contractor-grade alternatives.
