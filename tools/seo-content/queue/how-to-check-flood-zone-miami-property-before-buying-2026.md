## What the FEMA Flood Zone Letters Actually Mean for Miami Buyers

FEMA divides flood risk into letter-coded zones printed on Flood Insurance Rate Maps (FIRMs). In Miami, you will most commonly encounter four: Zone AE, Zone VE, Zone AH, and Zone X. Zone AE covers land at or below the 1-percent-annual-chance flood elevation — commonly called the 100-year floodplain — with detailed base flood elevation studies completed. This is the most common high-risk designation in inland Miami neighborhoods. Zone VE is the coastal high-velocity zone, meaning wave action is expected in addition to flooding; properties in VE face the highest NFIP premiums and the strictest building code requirements. Zone AH indicates shallow ponding flood risk, common in areas near the Miami River and older drainage corridors. Zone X is the moderate-to-minimal risk zone; flood insurance is not federally required here, but about 25 percent of all NFIP claims nationally come from Zone X properties, which tells you something important about what 'low risk' actually means in South Florida.
