## The honest answer on what goes viral in real estate content in 2026

Real estate content that goes viral is **never about the property**. It's about:
1. **A real person in real trouble** — buyer/seller in a specific bind, with a name, with stakes
2. **The villain is named** — predatory agent, hidden defect, insurance company, FEMA gap
3. **The reveal is visceral** — DNA score dropping, flood zone overlay, permit history, comp spread
4. **The save is human** — buyer gets to walk away / negotiate / make the better choice
5. **The take-action close is one tap** — "run a free report on any address"

The mistake everyone makes is leading with the tool. The right move is leading with the **person being saved**, and the tool is the supporting cast.

## The 7 content formats that work (ranked by reach × conversion)

### 1. Real-buyer save stories — long-form (8-12 min YouTube)
Format: Dan + an actual buyer who used PropertyDNA, walked away from a bad deal, or renegotiated using the data. **Real names, real addresses, real numbers.** "Maria was about to sign on a house in Indio. PropertyDNA flagged 4 unfinaled permits and a Citizens-only zone. Here's what happened next."

Why it works: Single human face + real stakes + your data shown on screen.
Production: 1 hour to film (interview), 2 hours to edit. Drives the most enterprise + agent inbound. THESE convert.

### 2. "Walk With Me Through Bad Listing" — Short (45-60s)
Format: Dan walks toward a Zillow listing on screen, narrates the listing's pitch, then opens PropertyDNA on the same address and reveals the gaps. ALWAYS land on a verdict (BUY / HOLD / WALK).

Why it works: Curiosity → revelation → verdict. Algorithm loves curiosity hooks. The verdict closes the loop.
Production: 30 min total. Pure phone-vertical. Five of these / week is the floor.

### 3. "What Your Agent Won't Tell You" — Skit (60s)
Format: Two-person bit. Dan + a buyer (could be the assistant on camera). The buyer asks a question, Dan plays "the agent" giving the bad answer, then plays "PropertyDNA" giving the real answer. Comedic tone, gold accents on the "PropertyDNA" cuts. 

Why it works: Repeatable format, fast cycles, surprises with each pain-point variant. Sketch comedy meets education.
Production: 20-30 min/skit. Cast the buyer once and reuse them as the foil.

### 4. Reactions to real listings posted by other agents — Short (45-60s)
Format: Dan reacts to a real Zillow / Realtor.com listing screenshot, narrates what the listing claims, then narrates what PropertyDNA's data on that exact address says.

Why it works: REAL data on REAL listings creates urgency + virality. Anchors the brand to specific local listings buyers are seeing.
Production: 15 min/clip. Pure phone-vertical. **CAUTION:** stay factual. Never disparage the agent personally. Lead with "the data on this address shows…" not "this agent is hiding…"

### 5. "PropertyDNA Saves The Humans" mini-commercial (15-30s)
Format: Cinematic short. Open with a couple walking into their dream house, smiling. Cut to flood gauge rising in their living room. Cut to PropertyDNA badge appearing on the listing. Cut to them walking away holding hands. Same shot, different ending.

Why it works: Pure emotion + brand identity. Tone like the Apple "Misunderstood" Christmas ad. Use sparingly (1/month). NO dialogue. Cormorant Garamond text. Save-the-humans tagline.
Production: 1 day shoot, 1 day edit. THIS is where you hire help.

### 6. Live property scans — Live stream or 30 min YouTube
Format: Dan opens a property report live on screen, walks through every signal, takes viewer-submitted addresses in real time.

Why it works: Authentic, interactive, builds community, repurposed into endless Shorts later.
Production: 30-45 min unedited, then clip out 5-8 shorts. Run once/week.

### 7. "Stale Listings That Should Sell" — Short series
Format: Dan picks a stale listing in a major desert club, narrates what PropertyDNA's data says about why it's not moving + what should change in the pricing/marketing. **This is a lead magnet for the referral network play.** Listing agents see it and contact you to take it over.

Why it works: Specific addresses, specific feedback, real expertise on display. Other agents see it.
Production: 15 min/clip. Tagged with the club + DOM count. Mentions stuartteamps@gmail.com at the end for referral inquiries.

## The viral cheat codes

1. **First 3 seconds determine everything.** Lead with: "Your agent isn't telling you about THIS on the home you're about to buy." Not "Hey guys."
2. **Pattern interrupt at 0:08.** Cut to a different angle / location / piece of B-roll. Algorithm rewards motion.
3. **Show real money on screen.** "$28,000 they would have spent on the wrong house." Specific numbers > round numbers.
4. **End with a verdict.** BUY / HOLD / WALK. Closure = saves.
5. **One CTA per video.** Either "run a free report" OR "drop an address in the comments" OR "download the iOS app." Never 3 CTAs.
6. **Captions on every video.** 80%+ of TikTok / Reels viewers are muted. Captions = the floor.
7. **Vertical 9:16 always.** Even for YouTube — Shorts is the discovery engine; full-screen long-form has half the algorithmic reach Shorts does.

- **"The Cherry-Picker"** — Dan plays an agent showing 3 cherry-picked comps. Cut to the algorithm showing 17. Repeat with louder sound effect. Sketch.
- **"The Flood-Zone Magician"** — Dan plays an agent making the flood-zone disclosure disappear with stage magic. Cut to PropertyDNA showing the AE designation. Sketch.
- **"The Insurance Reveal"** — Cold open: couple gets binder quote. Couple cries. Cut to PropertyDNA showing they could have seen Citizens-only at offer time. Cut to second couple smiling because they used PropertyDNA.
- **"What Your Agent Sees / What You See"** — Split screen. Agent's view = giant commission counter. Your view = an obscured listing with question marks. PropertyDNA pulls the curtain back.
- **"Stale Listing Therapist"** — Dan plays a therapist. Stale listing is the patient on the couch. "Tell me about your DOM count." Comedy + education.
