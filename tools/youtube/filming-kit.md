## Setting / Location

### Best option — your home office or study
- Warm wood tones in background = trust, authority, "I'm not in a startup hoodie"
- A bookshelf with REAL books visible (not props) reinforces "actual expert"
- A real estate market/architecture book or two on the visible shelf = on-brand subtext
- Pre-clear the desk — laptop closed, one notebook, one pen, a coffee/tea cup. Nothing else.

### Backup — Palm Springs / desert exterior at golden hour
- For the Thunderbird video specifically: 1-2 cutaway shots of the actual property exteriors (if you can drive past) are gold for the cold open
- Drive past 40380 Tonopah and 70629 Boothill, shoot 10 seconds vertical of the gates from public road = legal, no permit needed
- Golden hour = ~5:30-6:30 PM in June. Light is forgiving and on-brand desert luxury

### What NOT to do
- ❌ Don't film in a coffee shop or anywhere with background chatter
- ❌ Don't film in a car (looks low-budget, sounds bad)
- ❌ Don't film against a blank white wall (looks like a startup hostage video)
- ❌ Don't film standing — sit with hands on the desk. Authority is seated.

## Wardrobe

### What to wear (for ALL 5 videos in one shoot)
- **Top:** Dark navy or charcoal blazer over a crisp white or pale blue button-down. NO tie. Top button open.
- **Why:** Reads "broker / advisor" not "agent / salesman." Buyers trust the advisor look. Open collar = approachable.
- **Watch:** Visible if you have a nice one. Subtle signal of seriousness.
- **Glasses:** If you wear them, keep them on for credibility. Take off if you don't normally wear them — looks forced.

### What NOT to wear
- ❌ T-shirt or polo (looks too casual for the topic — money/legal advice)
- ❌ Full suit + tie (looks like a 1990s real estate ad — opposite of "we're the disruptor")
- ❌ All black (drains warmth on camera, makes you look severe)
- ❌ Patterned shirt (creates moiré on video compression)
- ❌ Logo gear (especially competitor brands)

### Color theory for the brand
- The site uses Cormorant + #C9A84C gold + #0A0908 dark. Your wardrobe should COMPLEMENT, not match.
- Navy / charcoal / cream / soft blue = ON brand
- Bright red, pure black, neon = OFF brand

## Audio (the single highest-leverage thing)

**Bad audio kills more videos than bad anything else.** Viewers tolerate average video, never tolerate bad audio.

### Best option — lavalier mic
- Rode Wireless GO II (~$300) or DJI Mic Mini (~$170). Clip to inside of blazer lapel.
- Records direct to mic, you transfer to computer after.

### Second-best — your phone with the AirPods Pro mic
- AirPods Pro w/ noise cancellation. Lower quality than a lav but workable.
- Record using **Voice Memos** on iPhone in parallel, sync later in Descript.

### Don't use
- ❌ Built-in laptop mic (echoes, picks up keyboard)
- ❌ Built-in phone mic from arm's length (sounds tinny)
- ❌ Bluetooth speaker mic (compression artifacts)

### Room treatment (free)
- Close all windows
- Turn off HVAC / fan for the 90 min
- Put a soft blanket on the desk to dampen surface reflections
- Test with 10 seconds of "the agent works for the commission, we work for you" — play it back. If you hear room echo, move closer to the mic or add more soft surfaces.

## Camera + lighting

### Camera — phone is fine
- iPhone 13 or newer (you have a current iPhone). 4K 30fps.
- **Mount it horizontally for long-form** (Thunderbird + Florida)
- **Mount it vertically for Shorts** (3, 4, 5)
- Lock exposure + focus by tapping and holding on your face before recording

### Phone tripod
- Cheap is fine. ~$25-50. Manfrotto PIXI or any equivalent.
- Position camera at **eye level**, NOT looking down at you. Looking-down camera angle screams "amateur podcast."

### Lighting
- **Best:** One soft light source from window in front of you (not behind), filming during daytime
- **Second-best:** Single ring light or LED panel pointed at you from camera-direction
- **Avoid:** Overhead room lighting only (creates raccoon-eye shadows). If you must, point a desk lamp at the ceiling to bounce + soften

### Framing
- Frame: top of head just below top of frame, shoulders at bottom 1/3
- Look directly into the camera lens (NOT at yourself on the screen — at the lens dot)
- Body angled ~15° away from camera, head pivoted back — looks more natural than dead-on
- Hands visible on the desk (gestures matter, but stay above the desk)

## Energy / delivery

### The on-camera tone
- **You are not selling.** You are **explaining what most buyers don't know.** Tone: trusted advisor, mildly indignant on the buyer's behalf.
- Vary pace. Slow down on the hook, speed up through the data, slow down again on the verdict.
- "Pause for emphasis" beats "louder for emphasis" 9 times out of 10.

### The "money line" technique
- For each script, identify ONE line that needs to land. For #03: *"Anytime that spread is more than 8%, it's cherry-picking."* For #04: *"Zillow's algorithm has zero idea."* For #05: *"Average cost when this surfaces at closing: twelve thousand dollars."*
- Record that ONE line twice — once at normal pace, once slightly slower with an intentional half-second pause before it.
- I'll pick the best take in edit.

### When you flub
- Don't restart from the top. Pause 3 seconds, repeat the LAST sentence, keep going. I cut around the flub.

## The first time you sit down

Hit record on the camera. Don't look at the script.

Look into the lens. Say:

> "The agent works for the commission. We work for you."

Pause. Smile a tiny bit. Stop recording.

Watch it back. If you don't immediately feel: "yes, that's the guy I'd trust with my money" — adjust lighting, sit up straighter, soften your jaw, do it again.

When you get the take that gives you that feeling, save it. That's your **hero clip** — the 3-second loop that opens every video, every Short, every social post, until the channel has 10K subs.

Then start with Script #03 (60s, easy, low-stakes warm-up). Then #04. Then #05. Then jump to the long-form.

You'll be done in 90 minutes.
