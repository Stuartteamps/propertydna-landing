## App Review Notes (private — Apple reviewer only)

PROPERTYDNA v1.0.3 (build 26) — NATIVE MAP INTELLIGENCE, STILL FULLY FREE

This update enhances the native Map tab (MKMapView). When the map settles on an area, the app fetches public property data from our API and drops native annotation pins showing each home's estimated value; tapping a pin opens a native detail sheet (value, risk-adjusted value, confidence, basic stats). It also includes valuation/reliability improvements on the server.

This does NOT change the purchasing model:
- The app remains 100% free. There are NO in-app purchases, NO subscriptions, and NO paid or externally-purchased content unlocked anywhere in the app.
- Every screen — the map, pins, detail cards, and reports — is fully visible to every user at no cost. The client forces free-tier state on iOS regardless of any web subscription tied to the same email; there is no gated/blurred content and no prompt to purchase anything.
- Sign-in (Apple, Google, or 6-digit email code) only saves the user's free report; it never unlocks paid content.
- Sign in with Apple is offered wherever Google sign-in is offered (Guideline 4.0).

HOW TO TEST
1. Open the Map tab. Pan/zoom to Palm Springs, CA. Property pins with values appear; tap one to see its detail card. All free.
2. Sign in via "or use email," enter a reviewer email, retrieve the 6-digit code, and enter it. (Apple and Google sign-in also work.)
3. Search any U.S. address (e.g., "100 W Vista Chino, Palm Springs, CA") to view a complete property report — every section visible, free, no upsell.

No paid or external content is required to evaluate the app.
