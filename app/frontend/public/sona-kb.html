4. HOW A PROPERTYDNA REPORT WORKS

A user enters any property address in the Coachella Valley. The system simultaneously queries:

  • Riverside County Assessor records (ownership, parcel data, assessed value)
  • City permit databases via BuildZoom (every permit ever filed: pools, additions, remodels, unpermitted flags)
  • RentCast (comparable sales, automated valuation, market data)
  • FEMA flood maps (flood zone designation)
  • US Census ACS (neighborhood demographics and income)
  • NWS weather data (climate and seasonal patterns)
  • SpotCrime (safety and incident data)
  • Anthropic Claude AI (generates the narrative analysis)

All data is synthesized into a formatted report delivered in under 60 seconds. The report includes: property overview, full permit history, ownership timeline, automated valuation with comparable sales, neighborhood heat map positioning, and an AI-written narrative explaining what the data means for this specific property.

5. WHAT IS PERMIT HISTORY AND WHY IT MATTERS

A permit history is a complete record of every construction permit filed on a property since it was originally built. This includes: room additions, pool installations, guest casitas, kitchen remodels, electrical panel upgrades, re-roofing, and any modifications that required a city or county permit.

Why it matters: In the Coachella Valley, it is extremely common for properties to have additions, guest structures, or pools that were built without the required permits. Unpermitted work:

  • Must be disclosed to buyers during sale
  • Can affect lender financing (some loans exclude unpermitted square footage)
  • May require retroactive permitting or demolition if discovered
  • Affects insurance coverage for the additions
  • Can affect property valuation

PropertyDNA surfaces this information automatically from county and city records. Agents who know about permit issues before a listing appointment can address them proactively instead of reactively.
